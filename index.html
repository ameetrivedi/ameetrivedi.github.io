Amee Trivedi

I'll be joining UBC CS as a postdoc soon.

I’m a Final Year Ph.D. candidate at CICS, UMass Amherst, graduating in Spring 2021. For my PhD I worked in the areas of ubiquitous computing, passive sensing, and IoT. I work with large-scale, real-world data to build systems and applications that have real-world impact.

At UMass, I am very fortunate to be mentored by Prof. Deepak Ganesan.

I am very thankful to Prof. Jayshree Jagdale, my undergrad professor, for mentoring and encouraging me to pursue my interest in research.

For updated list of my publications please see my Google Scholar Profile.

Research Interests

I am interested in graph processing, graph storage, graph representation, graph analysis, ubiquitous computing, IoT, and passive sensing.

At heart, I am a kernel programmer so Operating Systems and Distributed Systems always interest me.

Internships

I interned at IBM, Research during Summer 2020.

In 2015 I interned at Oracle Labs, Burlington in the Penumbra project where I worked on COW, UNDO and REDO logging systems for Persistent Memory. Here, I got an amazing opportunity to work with Margo Seltzer and Virendra Marathe.
